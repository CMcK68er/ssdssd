John Lennon

After the Beatles broke up, John became an anti-war activist along with his wife Yoko Ono, demonstrating , staging bed ins and writing anti war songs. He released his first solo album in December 1970 called John Lennon Plastic Ono band.

Imagine was released in September 1971, it became his best selling album and topped both the British and American charts. George Harrison was involved with the making of Imagine. The album is featureed in the All Time Top 100 charts and has been reissued multiple time featuring unreleased demos and various outtakes.

In September 1972 Lennon released Sometime in New York City. The album was politically charged addressing issues such as sexism, colonialism, incarceration and racism. Its a double album including a live disc and featuring artists George Harrison, Keith Moon and Billy Preston.

Up to 1975 Lennon was releasing one album per year. The album Rock'n'Roll was a covers album was released featuring old rock songs from the 50s. The album reached no.6 in both England and America later certified Gold in both countries. The cover of the album features a photo of Lennon taken during the Beatles time in Hamburg, Germany.

Minds Games the fourth solo album released by John Lennon on 16th November 1973. The album continued Lennon's previous attempts to chronicle his life through his songs. The title track was released as a single at the same time as the album. It reached number 13 in the UK charts and number 9 in the USA.

Influences

Buddy Holly
Buddy Holly could play guitar and sing at the same time-not only strum but actually play the licks which really impressed The Beatles.
Little Richard
McCartney and Lennon often borrowed a formula that Little Richard had made his trademark: three chords played with a contagious, driving rhythm and blues feel.
Chuck Berry
During the formative years, some of The Beatles hits were covers of Chuck Berry's songs. And throughout the band's iconic career Berry's influence was on nearly every album, from "I Saw Her Standing There" off Please Please Me to "Back in the U.S.S.R." off the White Album.
