George Harrison

George Harrison who was the lead guitarist with The Beatles was also known as the quiet Beatle. Lennon and McCartney get lots credit for most of The Beatles songs but Harrison has contributed a fair amount of classics to The Beatles Collection. After the breakup of The Beatles Harrison went on to have a very successful solo career as well as co-founding the supergroup The Traveling Wilburys.

Harrison's first two solo releases didnt get much recognition,it was only in 1970 with the release of All Things Must Pass This album contained two discs of his songs and a disc of recordings of Harrison jamming with friends. It topped the charts on both sides of the Atlantic and produced a number 1 single My Sweet Lord.

In January 1972 Conert for Bangladesh was released. A triple live album recorded over two concerts at the Madison Square Garden in New York. It was here that Harrison and Starr performed together for the first time on a live stage since the Beatles broke up in 1966. Bob Dylan, Ravi Shankar, Ali Akbar Khan, Billy Preston, Leon Russell and Eric Clapton also feature on this album.

In June 1973 Harrison released Living in the Material World This album became Harrison's second number one album in the U.S.A. It also topped the charts in Canada and Australia while reaching number two in the U.K. Give Me Love (Give Me Hope) became an international hit single which was taken from this album.

In December 1974 Harrison released Dark Horse which was moving towards funk and soul genres and did not go down too well with the music critics. Dark Horse would also be the name of Harrison's soon-to-be-formed new label, as well as a metaphor for the underestimated Beatle who leaped artistically and commercially ahead of his three colleagues immediately after the Beatles' breakup.

Influences

Skiffle Music
Ringo became interested in Skiffle music through his friend Roy Trafford whom he befriended in his first job around 1957.
Lennon's Invitation
In 1962 Ringo Starr replaced the original Beatles drummer Pete Best on John Lennons invitation. George Martin and the other Beatles were not too impressed
Number Ones
Starr had two number 1 singles in the States in 1972 and 1973, Photograph written with George Harrison and You're Sixteen written with The Shermann Brothers.
