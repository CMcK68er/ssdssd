Ringo Starr

Ringo Starr born Richard Starky was prone to illness as a young boy and it was during a period in hospital, a childrens band was formed where he became fond of the drums. Ringo officially joined The Beatles in 1962, replacing Pete Best as the drummer for the band. After the Beatles breakup in 1970, Starr had an amazing solo career which has seen him play with numerous musicians over the years in his All Starr Band.

Starr releasesd his debut solo album titled Sentimental Journey which contained renditions of pre-rock standards with contributions from Quincey Jones, Maurice Gibb, George Martin and Paul McMartney. This album peaked at number 7 in the British charts and 22 in the U.S.A.

Scottie Moore who played with Elvis engineered Ringo's second album , Beaucoups of Blues although country influenced featuring renowned Nashville session musicians it became a commercial failure. In between albums Starr featured on albums by other musicians, John Lennon/Plastic Ono Band,and George Harrison.

Ringo became Starr's third studio album released in 1973. It reached number 7 in the English charts and and number 2 in the Billboard charts. All four Beatles participated on this album as well as numerous musician friends ie. Marc Bolan, members of the Band, Billy Preston, Harry Nilsson amongst others.It was reported by the media at the time that these recording sessions were a Beatles reuniuon but turned out to be a rumour Photograph and You're Sixteen became big hits from this album.

Goodnight Vienna Starr's fourth album was released in 1974. Ony You, another cover single was released from the album and reached number one the easy listening charts early 1975. Once again old friends John Lennon, Billy Preston, Elton John and Robbie Robertson also featured on this album.

Influences

Skiffle Music
Ringo became interested in Skiffle music through his friend Roy Trafford whom he befriended in his first job around 1957.
Lennon's Invitation
In 1962 Ringo Starr replaced the original Beatles drummer Pete Best on John Lennons invitation. George Martin and the other Beatles were not too impressed
Number Ones
Starr had two number 1 singles in the States in 1972 and 1973, Photograph written with George harrison and You're Sixteen written with The Shermann Brothers.
