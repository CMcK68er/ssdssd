The Beatles

The Beatles The Greatest Rock Band in the world hailed from Liverpool, England in 1960. They comprised of John Lennon, Paul McCarthney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. The Beatles recorded together for a little over seven years. Between October 1962 and may 1970, they released thirteen albums and a number of tracks issued on standalone singles.

This catalogue created in the short period has sold more than that of any other group in history and its commercial success continues.

The groups significance stems not just from huge sales figures. Their Music music has inspired generation upon generation of musicians, songwriters and producers.

The Beatles got their influences from Bill Haley, Little Richard, Buddy Holly, The Everly Brothers and Eddie Cochran amongs others.

The Beatles seventh album Revolver released August 1966 was the most ambitious pop music committed to vinyl.What makes Revolver such an important and brilliant album is it introduces us all to phychedelic music. All four members contributed songs to Revolver.

Influences

Buddy Holly
Buddy Holly could play guitar and sing at the same time-not only strum but actually play the licks which really impressed The Beatles.
Little Richard
McCartney and Lennon often borrowed a formula that Little Richard had made his trademark: three chords played with a contagious, driving rhythm and blues feel.
Chuck Berry
During the formative years, some of The Beatles hits were covers of Chuck Berry's songs. And throughout the band's iconic career Berry's influence was on nearly every album, from "I Saw Her Standing There" off Please Please Me to "Back in the U.S.S.R." off the White Album.
